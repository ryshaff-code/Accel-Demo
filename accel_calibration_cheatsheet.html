What Each Instrument Characterizes

Centrifuge

  • Scale factor nonlinearity (K2, higher-order terms)
  • Asymmetry (K1+ vs K1−)
  • g² / anisoelastic sensitivity under high-g loading
  • Cross-axis response at elevated g
  • Full-range model validation beyond ±1g gravity limit
Note: Bias and scale factor (K0, K1) are more accurately determined by static multiposition tumble, per IEEE 836.

Rate Table / Indexer

  • Bias (K0) — zero-g output offset
  • Scale factor (K1) — gain accuracy
  • Input axis misalignment (Mip, Mia)
  • Axis non-orthogonality (cross-axis sensitivity)
  • Temperature sensitivity of all above (with thermal chamber)
Method: 6-position (minimum) or 24-point tumble test. 2-axis table calibrates all 3 axes in one setup.

Optical Metrology

  • Theodolite: Table rotation axis vs. gravity; fixture-to-table offset; cube face orthogonality
  • Autocollimator: Per-position platen wobble; automated per-position orientation correction
  • Decouples fixture misalignment from intrinsic sensor IA misalignment
  • Closes calibration traceability chain for orientation
Priority: Theodolite alone covers setup characterization and fixture/sensor decoupling. Autocollimator adds per-position automation.

Key Procurement & Integration Considerations

Before You Specify

  • Error budget first — assign allowable calibration uncertainty to each model term (K0, K1, K2, Mip, Mia) before writing equipment specs
  • Encoder accuracy vs. resolution — always ask for accuracy including wobble and runout, not just encoder resolution
  • Centrifuge radius — confirm thermal expansion management of arm (Invar preferred)
  • Axis orthogonality of table's own axes — errors here appear as apparent sensor misalignment

Facility & Infrastructure

  • Vibration isolation — both instruments require isolated foundations; centrifuge may need exclusion zone
  • Thermal stability — ±0.5°C or better for metrology-grade work; air column thermal gradients affect optical measurements
  • Lab layout — autocollimator needs clear, unobstructed line of sight to cube; plan sightlines before finalizing floor plan
  • Permanent survey monuments — install for repeatable theodolite repositioning between campaigns

Compliance & Traceability

  • ITAR/EAR — nav-grade test equipment is controlled; foreign national access and export require compliance review early in procurement
  • NIST traceability — centrifuge ω and radius, table angular position, and autocollimator must all have documented calibration chains
  • Annual calibration — budget downtime and cost; theodolite verified via 360° closure checks
  • Standards — IEEE 836-2009 (centrifuge), IEEE 1293-2018 (accelerometer test procedures)

Error Budget Workflow: Define allowable calibration uncertainty for each term above before writing tester specs. Tester accuracy should be ≤ 1/3 to 1/5 of the sensor's allowable error for that term (test uncertainty ratio). Terms driven by the rate table (K0, K1, Mip/Mia) set your indexer spec; terms driven by the centrifuge (K2, Ki) set your centrifuge spec. Optical metrology (theodolite) is foundational to both by anchoring the orientation reference.

Thermal Error Modeling & Characterization Reference

IEEE 1293-2018 Thermal Extensions  ·  Test Methods  ·  Thermal Chamber Specifications

IEEE 1293-2018
MIL-STD-810 · NIST Traceable
IEEE 1293 Thermal Model Extension
Base model coefficients expanded as polynomial functions of temperature — first and second order shown
K0(T)  =  K0₀  +  K0T·ΔT  +  K0T2·ΔT²      [ bias thermal model ]
K1(T)  =  K1₀  +  K1T·ΔT  +  K1T2·ΔT²      [ scale factor thermal model ]
ΔT  =  T  −  Tref      [ deviation from calibration reference temperature ]
Important: All thermal coefficients are defined relative to T_ref — the temperature at which the base calibration (K0₀, K1₀) was performed. A mismatch between T_ref and actual operating temperature compounds every other thermal error term. T_ref must be explicitly documented in the calibration record.
Thermal Model Term Definitions
Term Name Units Definition & Practical Notes
K0T Bias Temp. Coefficient µg/°C Linear rate of change of zero-g output with temperature. Dominant thermal error for most nav-grade sensors. Extracted from steady-state soak sweep.
K0T2 Bias Temp. Coef. (2nd order) µg/°C² Curvature of K0 vs. temperature. Needed for sensors with nonlinear thermal behavior; requires ≥ 5 temperature setpoints across range to resolve reliably.
K1T Scale Factor Temp. Coefficient ppm/°C Linear rate of change of gain with temperature. Must be extracted separately from K0T — data reduction must decouple the two terms at each temperature setpoint.
K1T2 Scale Factor Temp. Coef. (2nd order) ppm/°C² Curvature of K1 vs. temperature. Less commonly reported but significant for wideband temperature operating ranges (> 80°C span).
Hys Thermal Hysteresis µg Residual output difference when returning to T_ref after a full thermal excursion. Not captured by K0T — requires a dedicated up/down thermal cycle test. Often the largest single thermal error in practice. Caused by stress relief in mounting structures and mechanical creep.
S_trans Thermal Transient Sensitivity µg/(°C/min) Output error during active temperature change, not at steady state. Caused by thermal gradients across the sensor structure during ramp. Distinct from K0T — requires controlled ramp-rate test, not soak steps. Relevant for airborne or munition applications with rapid thermal transitions.
TOR Thermal Turn-On Repeatability µg (1σ) Variation in bias across repeated cold-start power cycles at the same temperature. Distinct from in-run bias stability (which assumes stable temperature). Driven by stress relaxation in sensor assembly and electronics settle. Requires statistical sample (n ≥ 10).
T_ref Calibration Reference Temperature °C The temperature at which K0₀ and K1₀ (base coefficients) are defined. All thermal model terms are relative to this point. Must match the intended operating condition or compensation model must account for the offset. Typically +20°C or +25°C for lab calibrations.

Test Method Summary

Term(s) Method Key Test Requirement

Thermal Chamber — Key Specifications for Nav-Grade Work

  • Temperature Range: −55°C to +85°C minimum for full mil-range; −40°C to +70°C for most commercial nav-grade programs
  • Setpoint Stability: ±0.1°C or better at steady state — stability, not just accuracy; sensor data collected only after stability confirmed
  • Ramp Rate: Programmable and repeatable 0.5–5°C/min; maximum rate spec alone is insufficient — controllability at low rates matters for transient sensitivity testing
  • Spatial Uniformity: ≤ 0.5°C gradient across DUT footprint; large gradients across sensor body drive apparent transient sensitivity even at steady state
  • Rate Table Integration: Pass-through for signal cables (slip ring or feedthrough); confirm cable thermal rating; optical window if autocollimator monitoring required during thermal test
  • Thermal Time Constant: Know your sensor's thermal time constant — soak time must be ≥ 3–5× this value; for quartz pendulous sensors this can be 20–40 minutes
  • NIST Traceability: Chamber temperature sensor calibrated with traceable reference; document calibration uncertainty in test record
Common Pitfall — Hysteresis vs. K0T: Thermal hysteresis is frequently misattributed to a large K0T value. If K0 measured after a thermal cycle does not match the pre-cycle value at T_ref, the discrepancy is hysteresis — not temperature coefficient. The two must be separated by test design: always record output at T_ref before and after each thermal excursion.
