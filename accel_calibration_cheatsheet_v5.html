What Each Instrument Characterizes

Centrifuge

  • K2 — scale factor nonlinearity (quadratic term)
  • Asymmetry — K1+ vs K1− at positive and negative g
  • Ki — anisoelastic sensitivity (a_p·a_a cross-product term)
  • Cross-axis response at elevated acceleration levels
  • Full-range model validation beyond the ±1g gravity limit
Note: Bias (K0) and scale factor (K1) are most accurately determined by static multiposition tumble per IEEE 836 — not centrifuge.

Rate Table / Indexer

  • K0 — bias, the zero-g output offset
  • K1 — scale factor gain accuracy
  • Mip, Mia — input axis misalignment (pendulous and output axes)
  • Non-orthogonality — cross-axis sensitivity between sensor axes
  • Thermal coefficients of all above when paired with a thermal chamber
Method: 6-position minimum or 24-point tumble test. A 2-axis table covers all 3 accelerometer axes in a single setup without repositioning.

Optical Metrology

  • Theodolite: Table rotation axis vs. local gravity; fixture-to-table angular offset; optical cube face orthogonality verification
  • Autocollimator: Per-position platen wobble detection; automated high-resolution orientation correction at each index position
  • Decouples fixture misalignment from intrinsic sensor IA misalignment
  • Anchors the NIST-traceable orientation reference chain for all calibration data
Priority: Theodolite alone covers setup characterization and fixture/sensor decoupling and is the recommended starting point before adding autocollimator.

Key Procurement & Integration Considerations

Before You Specify

  • Error budget first — define allowable calibration uncertainty for each model term before writing equipment specs; tester accuracy should be 1/3–1/5 of sensor allowable error per term (test uncertainty ratio)
  • Encoder accuracy vs. resolution — always request accuracy including wobble and runout, not just encoder resolution; the two numbers differ significantly
  • Centrifuge arm expansion — confirm thermal expansion management; Invar arms preferred for sub-100 ppm radius stability across operating temperature
  • Table axis orthogonality — errors in the table's own axes appear directly as apparent sensor misalignment in multi-axis tumble data reduction

Facility & Infrastructure

  • Vibration isolation — both instruments require isolated foundations; centrifuge may need a dedicated safety exclusion zone per facility safety requirements
  • Thermal stability — ±0.5°C or better for lab environment; air column thermal gradients between autocollimator and optical cube introduce beam steering errors that appear as angular measurement noise
  • Lab sightlines — plan theodolite and autocollimator positions before finalizing floor layout; install permanent survey monuments (tooling balls or kinematic mounts) for repeatable instrument repositioning
  • Cable pass-through — confirm slip ring current rating and cable thermal rating for integrated thermal chamber operation

Compliance & Traceability

  • ITAR/EAR — navigation-grade test equipment is export-controlled; foreign national access and any hardware export require early compliance review and appropriate licensing
  • NIST traceability — centrifuge angular velocity and radius, table angular position, and all temperature sensors must have documented calibration chains traceable to NIST
  • Annual calibration — budget instrument downtime and recalibration cost; theodolite verified via 360° closure checks; autocollimator via NIST-traceable optical polygon
  • Governing standards — IEEE 836-2009 (centrifuge test procedures), IEEE 1293-2018 (accelerometer test procedures and model equation specification)

Optical Metrology — Role & Integration

Theodolite — Setup & Periodic Survey

  • Table axis vs. gravity — establishes rotation axis tilt relative to local vertical; this systematic error aliases uniformly into every tumble position and must be corrected in data reduction
  • Fixture-to-table angular offset — measures the offset between the optical cube reference frame and the table mechanical frame at the reference index position; recorded as a known correction to separate fixture misalignment from sensor IA misalignment
  • Cube face orthogonality — verifies manufacturer orthogonality specification under actual lab thermal conditions before relying on the cube as an angular reference
  • Periodic re-survey — after table maintenance, fixture rebuild, or significant lab thermal event; repositioned to permanent survey monuments for inter-campaign repeatability
  • Recommended starting point — theodolite alone provides the orientation anchor needed to decouple fixture from sensor IA misalignment without autocollimator investment

Autocollimator — Per-Position Runtime Monitoring

  • Fixed externally in lab frame — reads the optical cube riding on the fixture; independent of table encoder, providing a second measurement channel for orientation truth
  • Per-position angle correction — digital angle output logged at each index position and substituted for nominal encoder angles in the misalignment fit; significantly tightens fit residuals on tables with 10–30 arc-sec encoder accuracy
  • Platen wobble detection — catches dynamic tilt of the rotating platen that the table encoder cannot observe; important for tables with modest bearing specifications
  • Test automation integration — electronic autocollimators output RS-232 or USB digital angle data; integrates into automated tumble test sequences without operator intervention at each position
  • Distinction from theodolite — narrow field, differential, high-resolution (±10–30 arcmin range); the theodolite establishes macro geometry once; the autocollimator monitors it at high resolution during every test run
Calibration & Characterization Workflow Summary

Phase 1 — Lab Setup & Geometric Reference

  • Level rate table using precision electronic tilt sensors (0.1 arc-sec class)
  • Mount optical cube on fixture co-located with sensor; bond to sensor case reference surface with rigid, thermally stable adhesive
  • Theodolite survey — establish table rotation axis tilt vs. local vertical; measure cube face orientation relative to table axis; verify cube orthogonality. Record all offsets as fixed corrections for data reduction
  • Install permanent survey monuments so theodolite can be repositioned consistently between campaigns

Phase 2 — Static Calibration (Rate Table)

  • 6-position or 24-point tumble — extract K0, K1, Mip, Mia via least-squares fit to gravity projections at known orientations
  • Temperature sweep — repeat tumble at ≥ 5–10 temperature setpoints; extract K0T, K1T; allow ≥ 30–60 min soak per setpoint; confirm ±0.1°C chamber stability before collection
  • Thermal hysteresis test — full up/down thermal cycle; record output at T_ref pre- and post-cycle to separate hysteresis from K0T
  • Allan Variance — stationary DUT at stable temperature; extract bias instability and ARW from log-log plot

Phase 3 — Dynamic Characterization (Centrifuge)

  • Index DUT using sub-table to place input axis radially; verify alignment with theodolite or autocollimator before spin-up
  • Multi-g sweep — run centrifuge at multiple ω settings covering full sensor range and beyond; extract K2 and asymmetry from quadratic fit to residuals after removing K0 and K1
  • Multi-orientation high-g runs — reindex DUT to multiple orientations to separate Ki·a_p·a_a (anisoelastic) from K2·a_in² (nonlinearity) geometrically; IA radial vs. tangential is the key orientation pair
  • Temperature-soaked centrifuge runs — for K2 vs. temperature characterization; allow full thermal equilibration before each run; sequential runs preferred over active ramp

Data Reduction — Key Relationships & Notes

Tumble Test (Rate Table)

  • K0 extraction: K0 = ½(V+g + V−g) — average of ±g pair; scale factor cancels exactly
  • K1 extraction: K1 = ½(V+g − V−g) / g — difference normalized to gravity reference
  • Mip, Mia: Cross-axis gravity projections at known orientations excite misalignment terms; extracted via least-squares fit across full orientation set
  • Use corrected orientation angles — substitute theodolite-measured offsets for nominal encoder angles as gravity projection reference; residuals tighten significantly

Centrifuge

  • Centripetal acceleration: a = ω²·r  |  δa/a = 2·δω/ω + δr/r — ω stability is the dominant uncertainty term
  • K2 extraction: Quadratic fit to (V − K0 − K1·a) vs. a²; residual computed relative to the tumble-derived linear model — decouple K0 and K1 first
  • Ki separation: Run DUT with IA radial vs. tangential; Ki·a_p·a_a appears only under simultaneous cross-axis loading — geometrically separable from K2·a_in² by orientation selection

Key Principles

  • Thermal decouple: Run full ±g pair at each temperature setpoint to extract K0(T) and K1(T) independently; fitting both from a single-axis measurement confounds the two
  • Test uncertainty ratio: Tester accuracy ≤ 1/3 to 1/5 of sensor allowable error per term; document TUR for each parameter in the calibration record
  • Ellipsoid fitting: For multi-axis IMU calibration, nonlinear least-squares fit to a 3D ellipsoid simultaneously recovers bias vector, scale factor matrix, and misalignment matrix from multi-position tumble data

Thermal Term Definitions
TermNameUnitsDefinition & Practical Notes
K0TBias Temp. Coef.µg/°CLinear rate of change of zero-g output with temperature. Dominant thermal error for most nav-grade sensors. Extracted from steady-state soak sweep. Second-order term K0T2 (µg/°C²) captures curvature; needed for ranges > 60°C span.
K1TScale Factor Temp. Coef.ppm/°CLinear rate of change of gain with temperature. Must be extracted separately from K0T — data reduction decouples the two at each setpoint. Second-order K1T2 (ppm/°C²) significant for ranges > 80°C.
HysThermal HysteresisµgResidual output difference returning to T_ref after a full thermal excursion. Not captured by K0T. Often the largest practical thermal error; caused by stress relief and creep in mounting structures.
S_transTransient Sensitivityµg/(°C/min)Output error during active temperature ramp, not at steady state. Caused by thermal gradients across sensor body. Requires controlled ramp-rate test; distinct from K0T. Critical for airborne and munition applications.
TORTurn-On Repeatabilityµg (1σ)Bias variation across repeated cold-start power cycles at the same temperature. Distinct from in-run bias stability. Driven by stress relaxation and electronics settle. Requires n ≥ 10 cycles.
T_refCal. Reference Temp.°CTemperature at which K0₀ and K1₀ are defined. All thermal terms are relative to this point. A mismatch between T_ref and operating temperature compounds every other thermal error. Document explicitly; typically +20°C or +25°C.

Thermal Chamber — Key Specifications

Required for Nav-Grade Thermal Characterization

  • Temperature Range: −55°C to +85°C (mil-range); −40°C to +70°C (most commercial nav-grade)
  • Setpoint Stability: ±0.1°C or better — stability, not just accuracy; data collection begins only after stability is confirmed
  • Ramp Rate Control: Programmable 0.5–5°C/min; max rate alone is insufficient — low-rate controllability required for transient sensitivity testing
  • Spatial Uniformity: ≤ 0.5°C across DUT footprint; gradients drive apparent transient sensitivity even at steady state
  • Rate Table Integration: Cable pass-through port; confirm cable thermal rating; optical window if autocollimator monitoring during thermal test
  • Soak Time: ≥ 3–5× sensor thermal time constant; quartz pendulous sensors may require 20–40 min per setpoint
  • NIST Traceability: Chamber temperature sensor on traceable calibration chain; uncertainty documented in test record
Pitfall — Hysteresis vs. K0T: If output at T_ref after a thermal cycle does not match the pre-cycle value, the discrepancy is hysteresis — not temperature coefficient. Always record output at T_ref before and after each excursion to separate the two effects.
